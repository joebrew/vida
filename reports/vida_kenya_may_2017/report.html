The goal

  • Harness verbal autopsy data to better understand causes of mortality in one place at one time (make compatible with SmartVA).
  • Make data compatible in one place across time.
  • Make data compatible across time and place.



The problem

Data babel

Data babel

Diversity

  • Diversity in content (the questions asked and answers collected)
  • Diversity in form (the coding of those questions and answers)

Why

  • Many verbal autopsy datasets do not conform fully to the international standards of the time of creation
  • Those very standards have changed over time

VIDA



The solution

We can fix form, not content

Content

We can't fix.

Form

We can.



The methods

Two end languages

  • Another standard (different variables and values, ie, "computer-speak")
  • Meaningful phrases (questions and answers, ie "human-speak")

Translation to another standard

  • Generally, the purpose wil be to prepare data for SmartVA.
  • Can also be used to combine datasets of different standards for ad-hoc analysis.

Translation to meaningful phrases

  • "child10019" becomes "What was the sex of the deceased?"
  • 1 becomes "female"
  • 2 becomes "male"

Two kinds of translation

  • The "clean" method
  • The "fuzzy" method

The clean method

  • Translation is easiest when we are translating to a known standard (language) to another known standard.

The clean method

  • But this means explicitly engineering each standard (dictionary writing)
{"Symptoms1":"symptoms", "Q527A":"q148b", "Q601A_3":"q600x"}
  • This is time-consuming and non-automatable

The fuzzy method

  • Employ approximate string matching to make a "best guess" at what each field is.
  • Imperfect, but fast.
  • Currently using Jaro-Winker distance.
  • Employing user confirmation will allow us to train a machine learning model to improve the algorithm's performance.
